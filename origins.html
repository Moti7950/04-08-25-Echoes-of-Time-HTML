History of Israel

Bronze Age Hellenistic Period

The Bronze Age

The Bronze Age (3300–1200 BCE) marks the beginning of urbanization in the Land of Israel. This era saw the rise of fortified cities, complex societies, and trade routes connecting Egypt, Mesopotamia, and the Levant.

The Hellenistic Period

After the conquests of Alexander the Great in the 4th century BCE, Greek influence spread across the land. Cities like Caesarea were built, and Greek language and culture merged with local traditions.

The Roman Period

In 63 BCE, Roman general Pompey conquered the region. The Jewish people faced severe oppression, leading to revolts such as the Great Revolt (66–73 CE) and the Bar Kokhba Revolt (132–136 CE). This era ended with the destruction of the Second Temple.

What is Canaan?

Canaan was the ancient name for the Land of Israel before the Israelite conquest. It was inhabited by various peoples and known for its rich culture.

Who lived here before Israelite times?

Archaeological evidence shows that the region was inhabited by Canaanites, Amorites, Hittites, and other peoples before the arrival of the Israelites.
