Modern Israeli Culture

The cultural legacy of ancient Israel is reflected in today’s holidays, music, literature, and art. Traditions like Passover and Hanukkah are still celebrated, while new cultural expressions reflect both old values and modern life.

Education and Historical Memory

Schools in Israel and Jewish communities around the world teach about the history of the land, the Bible, and Jewish resilience. Museums like Yad Vashem and the Israel Museum preserve artifacts and stories.

National Identity

For many, Israel represents not just a physical place, but a symbol of survival, hope, and unity. The Hebrew language, revived in modern times, connects the present with the biblical past.

What languages are spoken in Israel?

Hebrew and Arabic are the official languages. English is widely used in business and education.

How is history taught in schools?

From kindergarten to high school, students learn about ancient history, Jewish texts, and modern Israeli society.
